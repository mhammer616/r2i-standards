Basic Modal Window Guidelines

A lot of our sites utilize Modal Windows to display additional information we don't want to appear in the body copy of the page. They can also be used to prompt the user for additional information like gating content and showing a login or form to gather information needed to supply the person with that content, a video, or just a prompt that something went wrong.

Our standard for this follows a modified approach to Bootstrap's method.

Bootstrap adds a ton of elements wrapping various pieces of the modal window. It also has various classes to change the size of the dialog that appears. These are all unnecessary to make the modal window function. In the examples, we'll explore stripped-down versions of the HTML that you can use as boilerplates.
