Basic Accordions Guidelines

A majority of our sites utilize accordions. Very similar to tabs, they are used to hide and show data that may take up valuable real estate on the page. They are also commonly used for Frequently Asked Questions so that you see questions and click to expand for the answer.

Below are a few examples of how Accordions should be setup, along with the JavaScript to make them work. Various toggles have been added with animations/transitions so that you can use the base setup that's right for your design.

Triangle Accordions

Triangles should only be pointing up and down. Triangles that point right indicate going to a new page due to their use on mobile devices.

Arrow Accordions

Arrows should only be pointing up and down. Arrows that point right indicate going to a new page due to their use on mobile devices.
